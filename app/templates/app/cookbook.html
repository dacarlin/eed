Design: using Foldit to make mutations

In this step, you will obtain Foldit, a program for designing proteins, load in a model of β-glucosidase B, and make mutations.

Hardware

  • Mac or Windows computer capable of running Foldit

Software

  • Foldit (for now, please email us for a license)
  • Macromolecular model of β-glucosidase B
    • Protein Data Bank structure of β-glucosidase B
    • Foldit parameters file for 4-nitrophenylglucopyranoside
    • Conformers for 4-nitrophenylglucopyranoside
    • Foldit constraint file

Instructions

  1. Download and install Foldit
  2. Load in the four files comprising the molecular model
  3. Make your mutations (see cheat sheet)
  4. (Optional: save a PDB of your mutant)
  5. Write down the mutant and your hypothesis

Day 1: Transform expression cells

Hardware

  • Dry bath pre-warmed to 42 C
  • Ice in ice bucket
  • Pipets: 2 and 20 and 200 and tips

Software

Concentration Amount per sample
2 mL tube 1
Chemically-competent E. coli BLR 20 μL
Plasmid >20 ng/μL 2 μL

Instructions

  1. In an ice cold Goog tube, mix 1 µL plasmid and 20 µL competent cells
  2. Incubate on ice for 10 minutes
  3. Incubate at 42 C for 60 seconds
  4. Incubate on ice for 60 seconds
  5. Add 200 µL media and recover at 37 C for 1 hour
  6. Pipet 200 µL recovered cells on to plate, add glass beads, shake, and get rid of beads
  7. Incubate plate overnight at 37 C
